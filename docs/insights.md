1. Academic performance remains consistently high.

Students demonstrate strong academic achievement across the entire reporting period. The overall average grade is above 9.0, while at least 50% of all grades fall between 8.4 and 10.0. Although a small group of lower-performing students exists, their impact on overall academic outcomes is limited.

2. Subject performance is highly consistent.

The difference between the highest- and lowest-performing subjects is only about 0.4 grade points, indicating balanced achievement across disciplines. No subject shows critically low results. However, Computer Science performs slightly below the overall school average and may benefit from additional investigation into curriculum design or instructional approaches.

3. Assessment methods contribute differently to academic outcomes.

Homework accounts for more than one-third of all recorded grades, highlighting the importance of continuous assessment. Exams represent only around 15% of assessments, meaning overall academic performance is driven primarily by students’ day-to-day work. Project-based assessments have the lowest average grade (8.80), while tests generate the largest number of failing grades. Projects also exhibit the highest failure rate relative to assessment volume. In contrast, quizzes combine strong performance with the lowest academic risk.

4. Seasonal patterns are visible throughout the academic year.

Periods of lower performance tend to occur near semester-end and spring assessment periods, suggesting that increased workload and examination pressure may negatively affect student outcomes.

5. Academic performance remains stable over time.

Despite moderate month-to-month fluctuations, average grades remain centered around 9.2 throughout the reporting period. The strongest Month-over-Month improvement occurred in April 2026. Year-to-Date analysis confirms that cumulative academic performance remains stable and predictable across the academic year.

6. Forecasting indicates continued stability.

Time-series forecasting suggests that average grades are likely to remain at current levels over the coming months. While the forecasted mean remains stable, the widening confidence interval indicates increasing uncertainty over longer forecasting horizons.
